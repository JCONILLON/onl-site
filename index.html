About

I'm Jean-Charles, a mastering engineer based in Paris. I got into this in 2020 and it quickly became the thing I spend most of my time on.

Technical Guidelines

How to prepare your track

01

File Format

Export as WAV or AIFF, 24-bit or 32-bit, at 44.1 kHz or higher. Normalize: Off. Dither: Off.

02

Headroom

Keep your master bus below 0 dBTP. Aim for peaks around −6 dBTP. Do not use a limiter to fix clipping — lower all tracks collectively instead.

03

Dynamic Control

Avoid over-compressing your mix. A well-controlled dynamic range gives the mastering more room to work, resulting in a punchier and more impactful sound.

04

Beginning & End

Leave at least 100 ms of silence at the start and 2 seconds at the end. This preserves transients and reverb tails during mastering.

05

No Mixbus Processing

Remove all compressors, EQs, stereo wideners, and limiters from the master channel. A clean signal allows for the most effective and high-quality result.

06

References & Notes

Send a reference track if you have a specific sound in mind. It helps me understand your vision and tailor the process to your expectations.

01

Clean & Edit

Remove unwanted noise, clicks, and pops. Trim silence at the start and end of each track and apply fades to avoid abrupt transitions.

02

Label Your Tracks

Name each track clearly: Vocals_Main, Kick_Drum, Guitar_Rhythm… Include BPM and key information if known.

03

Consolidate & Export

All tracks must start at bar 1. Export as WAV or AIFF, 24-bit or 32-bit, at 44.1 kHz or higher. Dither: Off. Normalize: Off.

04

No Processing

Remove all EQ, compression, reverb, and effects from each track. If an effect is essential (e.g. amp simulation), send both the wet and dry version. Keep peaks below −12 dB on the master.

05

Additional Materials

Provide a reference track or your own self-mix. Include session notes with specific instructions or creative directions for the mix.

06

File Transfer

Name your folder Artist Name – Track Name, zip it, and upload it to WeTransfer, Dropbox, or Google Drive. Send the link to onl.contact.off@gmail.com.
